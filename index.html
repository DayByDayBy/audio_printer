üéöÔ∏è Understanding the Controls

Seed (--seed, -s)

The seed is the DNA of your sound. It's a single number that determines everything about how your drone will sound. The same seed will always produce the exact same sound‚Äîthis is called deterministic generation. You can share seeds with friends like sharing recipes.

Try: Start with seed 42, then try 1337, 9999, or your birthday as YYYYMMDD.

Duration (--duration, -d)

How long your sound lasts, in seconds. Longer durations let slow modulations develop and evolve. Shorter ones are punchier.

Typical range: 15-60 seconds. Very long drones (2-3 minutes) can be meditative.

Voices (--voices, -v)

Each voice is an independent layer of sound. More voices = richer, more complex textures. Fewer voices = cleaner, more focused sounds.

Sweet spot: 3-7 voices. One voice can sound thin; 10+ voices might become muddy.

Output (--output, -o)

The filename for your generated WAV file. Use descriptive names!

Example: dark_ambient_42.wav or spaceship_hum.wav

üî¨ What's Actually Happening?

The synth generates "drone" sounds by layering multiple voices. Each voice contains:

  • An oscillator - the core tone generator (sine, saw, square waves, or noise)
  • LFOs (Low Frequency Oscillators) - slow modulations that make the sound "breathe" and evolve. They're oscillators just like the main one, but running much slower (below the range of pitch)
  • Filters - shape the tone by removing high or low frequencies
  • Envelopes - control how the sound fades in and out
  • Effects - distortion and reverb add character and space
Key insight: An LFO and an oscillator are doing the same job (oscillating), but at different speeds. Below about 20 Hz, we hear it as "wobble" or "modulation". Above 20 Hz, we hear it as "pitch". This is one of the beautiful fundamentals of synthesis!

üíª Command Line Usage

Argument Short Default Description
--seed -s 42 Random seed (any integer)
--duration -d 30.0 Length in seconds (float)
--voices -v 5 Number of layers (integer)
--output -o drone.wav Output filename

Basic Examples

# Generate with defaults (seed 42, 30 seconds, 5 voices)
uv run drone.py
# Custom seed and duration
uv run drone.py -s 1337 -d 45
# More voices for complexity
uv run drone.py -v 8 -o thick_texture.wav
# Complete custom sound
uv run drone.py --seed 20240315 --duration 60 --voices 6 --output my_drone.wav

üß™ Experimentation Tips

  • Start with the classics above to understand what different parameters feel like
  • Change one thing at a time - try the same seed with different voice counts
  • Use meaningful seeds - dates, favorite numbers, coordinates, etc.
  • Keep a notebook - write down seeds you like and what they remind you of
  • Think before you render - this is a sound printer, not a toy. Plan your sound!
  • Compare extremes - try seed 1 vs seed 1000000, or 2 voices vs 10 voices
  • Layer in a DAW - generate multiple drones and overlap them in Audacity or similar

üéì Going Further

Once you're comfortable with the basics, try:

  • Creating a series - Use sequential seeds (1000, 1001, 1002...) to make variations
  • Matching durations - Generate multiple 30-second pieces that can be cross-faded
  • Finding your palette - What range of seeds gives you the sounds you like?
  • Reading the code - Open drone.py and see how the oscillators and filters work
Remember: Every sound you generate is completely unique and reproducible. The seed is like a coordinate in infinite sound-space. You're not just making noise‚Äîyou're exploring a vast, deterministic universe of possibilities.

üêõ Troubleshooting

Command not found? Make sure uv is installed and in your PATH.

Generation takes too long? Reduce duration or voices. A 60-second, 10-voice drone can take a minute to render.

Sound is too quiet/loud? The synth normalizes output, but you can adjust levels in any audio editor.

Can't hear anything? Some seeds produce very low frequencies‚Äîtry with headphones or speakers with good bass response.
